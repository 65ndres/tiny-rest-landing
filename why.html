Why Tiny Rest

Sleep is the foundation for growth

Quality rest supports your baby's brain development, mood, feeding rhythm, and ability to learn throughout the day. When sleep is off, everything else can feel harder — for your little one and for you.

Research on infant sleep shows that babies fall asleep more easily and stay asleep longer when they are tired enough, but not overtired. The sweet spot depends on how long they have been awake — their wake window — and changes quickly as they grow.

Tiny Rest brings that science into everyday parenting. Log naps and feedings, see your child's timeline at a glance, and get a clear prediction for the next nap so you can plan your day with a little more calm and a lot less mental math.

Rest supports more than sleep

Consistent, well-timed sleep helps regulate your baby's circadian rhythm — their internal body clock — which in turn supports healthier night sleep, steadier moods, and better daytime alertness for learning and play.

During wake windows, activities like feeding, fresh air, floor time, free play, and face-to-face interaction all contribute to healthy development. When naps land at the right moments, your baby has the energy to engage, explore, and bond — and you get pockets of predictability back in your day.

Tiny Rest does not replace your pediatrician or a sleep consultant, but it gives you a clear record of what is actually happening — the first step toward a schedule that fits your child's age, temperament, and your family's life.

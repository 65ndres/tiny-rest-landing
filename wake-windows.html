Wake Windows

Wake windows, made simple

A wake window is the amount of time your baby is awake between one sleep period and the next — from the moment you get them out of the crib until you lay them down again. It includes feeding, play, fresh air, and your nap routine.

For babies under six months, experts recommend using wake windows together with sleepy cues like yawning, reduced activity, and droopy eyelids. After six months, many families also benefit from a more predictable, clock-based schedule — especially once their little one moves to two naps a day.

Keeping track of all of this is exhausting when you are already running on little sleep yourself. Tiny Rest remembers wake times, nap history, and feeding logs for you, so you can focus on your baby instead of the clock.

Avoid overtiredness before it starts

When wake windows stretch too long, babies can become overtired — fussy, hard to settle, and more likely to wake frequently. Wake windows that are too short can lead to short catnaps and bedtime battles.
